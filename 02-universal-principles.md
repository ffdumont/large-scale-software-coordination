# 2. Universal Principles

## Research question

Which principles appear consistently in high-performing large-scale software organizations, independently of the framework they use?

## Candidate principles

- alignment;
- transparency;
- autonomy with boundaries;
- fast feedback;
- responsibility and ownership;
- built-in quality;
- continuous improvement;
- cadence;
- flow visibility;
- WIP limitation;
- dependency reduction;
- evolutionary architecture;
- adaptive planning;
- organizational learning.

## Evidence to investigate

- DORA and Accelerate research;
- Lean product development;
- DevOps literature;
- queueing theory;
- Team Topologies;
- Kanban and flow metrics;
- organization theory;
- empirical experience reports from large technology organizations.

## Analytical stance

Principles are not ceremonies. They are recurring constraints or design heuristics that can be implemented in different ways.

The section should distinguish between:

- universal principles;
- context-dependent practices;
- framework-specific implementations;
- cultural preferences.
