# Annex A — Annotated Bibliography

> Status: initial seed. This bibliography will be expanded progressively.

## Software engineering and scale

- Brooks, Frederick P. *The Mythical Man-Month*. Addison-Wesley.
  - Key contribution: adding people to a late software project can make it later; communication and coordination costs grow with team size.

- Conway, Melvin E. "How Do Committees Invent?" 1968.
  - Key contribution: systems tend to mirror the communication structures of the organizations that design them.

- Parnas, David L. "On the Criteria To Be Used in Decomposing Systems into Modules." 1972.
  - Key contribution: information hiding and modularity as mechanisms to reduce coordination and change impact.

## Lean, flow, and queueing

- Goldratt, Eliyahu M. *The Goal*.
  - Key contribution: local optimization can reduce global throughput; bottlenecks govern system performance.

- Reinertsen, Donald G. *The Principles of Product Development Flow*.
  - Key contribution: queueing theory, batch size, WIP, and economic decision-making in product development.

## DevOps and empirical software delivery

- Forsgren, Nicole; Humble, Jez; Kim, Gene. *Accelerate*.
  - Key contribution: empirical link between technical practices, delivery performance, and organizational performance.

- DORA Reports.
  - Key contribution: ongoing empirical research on software delivery performance and capabilities.

## Organization theory and systems thinking

- Simon, Herbert A. *The Sciences of the Artificial*.
  - Key contribution: complex systems, hierarchy, bounded rationality, and design.

- Mintzberg, Henry. *Structure in Fives*.
  - Key contribution: organizational forms and coordination mechanisms.

- Senge, Peter. *The Fifth Discipline*.
  - Key contribution: learning organizations and systems thinking.

## Modern organizational patterns

- Skelton, Matthew; Pais, Manuel. *Team Topologies*.
  - Key contribution: team cognitive load, interaction modes, and organization design for fast flow.

- Kersten, Mik. *Project to Product*.
  - Key contribution: product-oriented flow and value stream thinking.
