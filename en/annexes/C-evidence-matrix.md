# Annex C — Evidence Matrix

## Purpose

This matrix connects claims, mechanisms, and sources. It is intended to prevent unsupported conclusions.

## Evidence levels

| Level | Type | Typical strength |
|---|---|---|
| A | Peer-reviewed research, established theory, replicated empirical evidence | Strong |
| B | High-quality empirical reports, longitudinal industry research | Strong to medium |
| C | Credible public case studies and conference talks | Medium |
| D | Official framework documentation | Useful for intent, weak for effectiveness |
| E | Practitioner opinion, vendor marketing, anecdote | Weak unless corroborated |

## Matrix

| Claim | Evidence level | Source | Confidence | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Communication and coordination costs grow with team size | A | Brooks; organization theory | High | Needs nuance: not all possible communication paths are active. |
| Technical coupling creates organizational coordination needs | A | Conway; Parnas; Team Topologies | High | Relationship is bidirectional. |
| Small batches and WIP limits improve flow | A/B | Reinertsen; Lean; Kanban literature | High | Empirical validation varies by context. |
| CI/CD reduces integration risk | A/B | DORA; Accelerate; DevOps literature | High | Requires technical discipline and architecture. |
| SAFe is intrinsically bureaucratic | E/C | Practitioner critiques | Low to medium | Needs careful distinction between design and implementation. |
