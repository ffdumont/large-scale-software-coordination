Two scores per function. The gap decides.

Each of the twelve coordination functions is rated twice, independently — how important it is, and how well it works today. Neither number means anything alone. Where they land together is the recommendation.

Cost overlay

A third question asks which two or three functions cost more than they are worth. It moves points, it does not create them.

Not important and costly → cut first.

Important and costly → fix it. Do not kill it.

Plotted per role

The same field is drawn for each role and each tenure band.

Where roles disagree, the disagreement is the finding. Teams scoring their own decision autonomy at 2 while managers score it at 4 is a diagnosis in itself — and usually more actionable than either number.

What it never asks

No scored item names a framework, ceremony, role or tool. Not one.

Every item describes a function — the job being done. Function is what we cannot lose without noticing. Form is what is on the table.
